Holter Monitor

What is it?

It is a battery operated portable device, which is used to measure, and records the activities (ECG) continuously from 24 to 48 hours or longer depending on the type of monitoring used. The size of the device is so small which can be attached on the body. The size of the electrodes is small as the 5-cents coin, which is attached to the skin. This Holter monitor and other devices, which are used to record the ECG, can be used during our daily activities and are called ambulatory electrocardiograms.

Quick Facts

  • Patients can be asked to wear Holter monitors to check whether they have slow, fast or irregular heartbeat. Or the doctor can use it to check how well the medicine given is working to treat the problems related to hearts.
  • This device has no risk and is not painful too.
  • This device helps the patients as well as the doctor decide whether need more tests or medicines for the heart.

Need Of Wearing Holter monitors

Doctors check up the heart and know the heart’s activity at one point in time during the ECG test. Sometimes, abnormal heart rhythms and cardiac symptoms may come and go. This is not possible to test at that particular time period so doctors provide this device to take the data of the condition of the heart for period of time.

Risk Of Using Holter monitors

Wearing this kind of monitor have no risks and causes no pain at all. These monitors have wires that are connected to small discs (electrodes) attached to the chest to record the electrocardiogram. The electrodes are attached with the tape or adhesive, which stuck on the chest.
